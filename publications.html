2022
Saccade-related modulation of oscillatory activity in primary auditory cortex
Barczak A, O'Connell MN, McGinnis T, Mackin K, Schroeder CE, Lakatos P
bioRxiv (preprint)
💡 Every time your eyes flick to a new location, the auditory part of your brain also changes its activity — suggesting eye movements help orchestrate how the brain processes sound, not just vision.
2020
Cross-species functional alignment reveals evolutionary hierarchy within the connectome
Xu T, … Schroeder CE, … Milham MP
NeuroImage (2020)
💡 The brain regions most similar between humans and monkeys are the basic sensory areas; the regions most different are the ones involved in complex thought. This evolutionary gradient tells us which human brain features are uniquely ours.
2019
Dynamic Modulation of Cortical Excitability during Visual Active Sensing
Barczak A, Haegens S, Ross DA, McGinnis T, Lakatos P, Schroeder CE
Cell Reports (2019)
💡 When an animal actively looks at something (versus passively receiving the same image), the brain processes visual information very differently. Active looking resets brain oscillations and amplifies responses — demonstrating that vision is an active act, not a passive one.
2014
Motor contributions to the temporal precision of auditory attention
Morillon B, Schroeder CE, Wyart V
Nature Communications (2014)
💡 Your body's motor system helps you listen more precisely — movements like nodding your head or tapping your foot help your brain lock onto the rhythm of speech and music. Hearing is partly a physical act.
2013
Mechanisms underlying selective neuronal tracking of attended speech at a "cocktail party"
Zion Golumbic EM, Ding N, Bickel S, Lakatos P, … Schroeder CE
Neuron (2013)
💡 When you focus on one voice in a noisy room, specific neurons in your brain physically lock onto the rhythm of that voice — and ignore others. This explains how attention works at a cellular level.
2009
Low-frequency neuronal oscillations as instruments of sensory selection
Schroeder CE, Lakatos P
Trends in Neurosciences (2009)
💡 A landmark review proposing that slow brain rhythms aren't background noise — they are fundamental tools the brain uses to selectively attend to the right information at the right time.
2008
Entrainment of neuronal oscillations as a mechanism of attentional selection
Lakatos P, Karmos G, Mehta AD, Ulbert I, Schroeder CE
Science (2008)
💡 Published in Science — one of the world's top journals — this paper showed that when you pay attention to a rhythmic stimulus, your brain's oscillations literally synchronize with it, boosting processing efficiency. Attention works by tuning the brain like a radio dial.

Volunteer Opportunities

Help us understand the brain

By participating in a study, you directly contribute to discoveries that may improve treatments for ADHD, depression, anxiety, and other conditions.

🧲

Brain Scan + EEG Study

Brain scan (MRI) while wearing a comfortable EEG cap. No injections, no radiation. You'll perform tasks or simply rest while we measure brain and body signals.

⏱ 2–3 hrs  ·  💰 Compensated  ·  📍 Orangeburg, NY
Express Interest
⚡

EEG-Only Study

A comfortable cap with small sensors on your scalp measures your brain's electrical signals. Completely non-invasive — just a cap. Perform cognitive tasks or rest.

⏱ 1–2 hrs  ·  💰 Compensated  ·  📍 Orangeburg, NY
Express Interest
💻

Online Studies

Some studies can be done from home — questionnaires about your thoughts, attention, and daily habits. Help us understand everyday cognitive experience.

⏱ 20–60 mins  ·  💰 Compensated  ·  🌐 Remote
Express Interest
Eligibility: Healthy adults aged 18–60, no neurological conditions. No metal implants for MRI studies. All data is de-identified and kept confidential. Participation is voluntary; you may withdraw at any time. Studies are conducted under IRB oversight at the Nathan Kline Institute.

Open Data & Computational Resources

Datasets and tools shared following FAIR principles for the neuroscience community.

🧠

NKI Rockland Sample

Lifespan neuroimaging dataset (fMRI, EEG, behavioral phenotyping, 1000 participants ages 6–85). Continuously updated and openly shared via INDI.

Access →
🐒

PRIME-DE

PRIMatE Data Exchange — global open NHP neuroimaging resource (fMRI, structural MRI) aggregated across labs worldwide. Core data infrastructure for the center.

Access →
🌐

HNN

Human Neocortical Neurosolver — open-source biophysical modeling tool for interpreting EEG/MEG signals via neocortical circuit models. Developed by the Jones Lab (Project 4).

Access →
🔧

C-PAC

Configurable Pipeline for the Analysis of Connectomes — open-source, high-throughput fMRI preprocessing and analysis platform. Developed by the Child Mind Institute.

Access →
